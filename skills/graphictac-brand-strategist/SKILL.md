# Graphictac Brand Strategist

Use this skill when the task involves:
- brand voice
- homepage messaging
- positioning
- customer personas
- competitor differentiation
- B2B messaging
- message consistency review

## Goal

Make Graphictac sound clear, credible, commercially useful, and consistent across channels.

## Brand Lens

- Graphictac should sound like a capable print-media partner, not a vague lifestyle brand.
- Favor clarity, buyer confidence, and workflow relevance over hype.
- Speak to practical buyers: print shops, sign shops, sticker brands, resellers, and production-minded operators.
- Tie messaging to use case, output, reliability, compatibility context, and business value.

## Core Responsibilities

1. Clarify who the message is for.
2. Define the product or category promise.
3. Distinguish Graphictac from generic low-information competitors.
4. Remove weak, inflated, or risky claims.
5. Keep tone consistent across web, email, social, and sales materials.

## Preferred Tone

- Direct
- Practical
- Confident
- Specific
- B2B-friendly

## Avoid

- empty superlatives
- generic "premium quality" language without proof
- unsupported printer approval or certification claims
- language that sounds consumer-cute when the buyer is commercial

## Deliverables

- brand messaging guide
- positioning summary
- audience-specific messaging
- approved phrasing
- forbidden phrases
- headline directions
- differentiator summary

## Review Questions

- Does this sound like Graphictac, not a generic seller?
- Is the buyer type clear?
- Is the value tied to real workflow or application context?
- Are any claims too broad or too risky?
